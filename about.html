ABOUT THE ISLAND

Welcome to Taniti Island

Taniti is a beautiful, tropical paradise located in the Pacific, known for its lush rainforests, stunning beaches, and a small, active volcano. With a population of around 20,000, the island maintains its natural charm while embracing a growing tourism industry. Whether you're looking to relax by the beach or explore the wild interior, Taniti offers something for every traveler.

A Natural Haven with a Rich Culture

Though Taniti covers less than 500 square miles, the island’s diverse landscape ranges from white sandy beaches to mountainous terrain. Visitors can enjoy a mix of adventures, from hiking in the rainforest to learning about the island's indigenous culture. Taniti’s economy, traditionally based on fishing and agriculture, now flourishes with tourism as its driving force.

Frequently Asked Questions

A: Taniti City has a reliable public bus system, while taxis and rental cars are available for exploring the rest of the island. Renting a bike is also a popular option for tourists.

A: Taniti is a safe destination, with violent crime being very rare. However, tourists should remain cautious of petty crimes such as pickpocketing, especially in crowded areas.

A: The best time to visit Taniti’s active volcano is during the dry season, when guided tours and helicopter rides are available. Always check for safety updates before planning your trip.

A: The U.S. dollar is the official currency on Taniti, but many businesses also accept euros and yen. Major credit cards are widely accepted, and currency exchange services are available at local banks.

A: Taniti uses 120-volt power outlets, the same as in the United States. Travelers from other countries should bring appropriate adapters if necessary to use their devices.
